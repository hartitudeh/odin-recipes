Unstuffed Cabbage Roll

Description

Made recipe exactly as written. This was a scrumptious, rich and hearty meal. Garnished with sour cream and flat leaf parsley.

Ingredient

  • Protein: Assorted meats (beef, goat meat, cow skin/kpomo, tripe/shaki), stockfish, and smoked/dried fish.
  • Egusi: Ground melon seeds.
  • Oil: Red palm oil.
  • Pepper Mix: Blended mix of fresh pepper (e.g., scotch bonnet/atarodo, bell pepper/tatashe) and onion.
  • Aromatics/Seasoning: Chopped onion, ground crayfish, locust beans (iru/ogiri, optional), seasoning cubes, and salt to taste.
  • Vegetables: Chopped leafy greens like fluted pumpkin leaves (ugu), spinach, or bitter leaf.
  • Liquid: Meat/fish stock (broth) or water.

Preparation Steps

      Step 1: Prepare the Meat and Stock
      • Cook Protein: Place your assorted meats (beef, goat meat, cow skin, etc.) in a pot. Add sliced onion, seasoning cubes, and salt.
      • Add Liquid: Pour in enough water to cook the meat and create a rich stock.
      • Boil: Cook until the meat is tender.
      • Reserve: Remove the cooked meat and fish from the pot, but keep the stock (broth).

      Step 2: Prepare the Egusi Paste and Pepper Base
      • Egusi Paste: In a bowl, mix the ground egusi seeds with a little chopped onion and a small amount of warm water or stock to form a thick, stiff paste. (This helps create the characteristic lumps when fried).
      • Boil Pepper (Optional): If your blended pepper mix is too watery, you can boil it in a separate pot to evaporate some of the liquid.

      Step 3: Fry the Egusi
      • Heat Oil: Place a clean pot over medium heat and add the palm oil. Allow it to heat for about 2-3 minutes until it's melted and hot (but do not bleach it).
      • Add Paste: Scoop the thick egusi paste into the hot palm oil. Do not stir immediately. Allow it to fry undisturbed for about 3-5 minutes so it begins to set and form lumps.
      • Fry and Stir: Begin to gently stir and break up the egusi as it fries. Continue to stir-fry for about 5-10 minutes until the egusi looks clumpy, slightly toasted, and its color has changed.

      Step 4: Build the Soup
      • Add Pepper and Aromatics: Pour the blended pepper mix, ground crayfish, and locust beans (if using) into the pot with the fried egusi. Stir well.
      • Simmer: Let it simmer for about 5-10 minutes, stirring occasionally.
      • Add Stock: Pour the reserved meat stock into the pot. Stir to combine. If the soup is too thick, add more water or stock until you reach your desired consistency.
      • Add Protein: Return the cooked meat, stockfish, and smoked fish to the soup.
      • Season: Add seasoning cubes and salt to taste. Allow the soup to simmer for another 10-15 minutes, letting the flavors meld.

      Step 5: Add Vegetables and Final Simmer
      • Add Greens: Stir in the chopped leafy greens (ugu, spinach, or bitter leaf). Cook for an additional 5 minutes until the greens are wilted and tender.
      • Final Taste: Taste the soup and adjust seasoning if necessary.
      • Serve: Remove from heat and serve hot with your choice of swallow (fufu, pounded yam, eba, etc.).

    Egusi Soup is traditionally served hot with a "swallow" like Pounded Yam, Garri (Eba), Semolina, or Fufu.
